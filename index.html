About

I study how bacteria, phages, and the host interact to shape gut ecosystems and influence health and disease.

I am a researcher working on microbial ecology, bacteriophages, and host–microbe interactions, with a focus on how microbial communities and phage-derived tools can be leveraged to interrogate and modulate the gut microbiota. My work combines wet-lab microbiology, genomics, and computational approaches to understand how specific microbes and their phages contribute to ecosystem function, cancer risk, and therapeutic responses.

I am particularly interested in precision microbiome engineering, including the development of phage-derived lysins and other targeted strategies to reshape microbial communities in a controlled and mechanistic way.

Keywords: gut microbiota, bacteriophages, lysins, cancer microbiome, microbial ecology, host–microbe interactions, genomics, bioinformatics.

Research

My research spans several interconnected themes:

  • Phage–bacteria interactions in the gut: How phages and phage-derived enzymes shape the structure and function of small-intestinal and colonic microbial communities.
  • Microbiome and cancer: Exploring how specific bacteria and microbial consortia contribute to colon and pancreatic cancer initiation, progression, and treatment response.
  • Precision microbiome engineering: Designing targeted interventions (e.g., phage lysins, tailored consortia) to selectively perturb or restore microbial ecosystems.
  • Computational pipelines for microbiome & virome data: Developing reproducible workflows for genome assembly, gene cluster annotation, functional profiling, and visualization.

Tools & resources

  • Custom pipelines for virome and microbiome analysis: Workflows for assembly, annotation, and visualization of phage and bacterial genomes (Snakemake / bash / Python / R).
  • Scripts for gene cluster extraction and visualization: Automation of BLAST-based region extraction, orientation, and Clinker-ready visualization of genomic neighborhoods.
  • Reproducible analysis notebooks: R and Python notebooks for statistical analysis, network inference, and figure generation.
