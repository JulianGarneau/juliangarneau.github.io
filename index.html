Hi!
I'm Julian

A microbiome and phage researcher based in Switzerland. I study how bacteria, bacteriophages and the host interact to shape gut ecosystems and influence health and disease, with a particular focus on small-intestinal communities, butyrate-producing taxa and cancer-associated microbiota. I combine wet-lab microbiology, genomics and computational approaches to develop precision microbiome engineering strategies, including phage-derived lysins to selectively remodel microbial communities.

About me

I work at the interface of microbiology, virology and computational biology to understand how microbial communities and their mobile genetic elements shape host physiology and disease risk. My current work and research interests include:

  • Phage–bacteria interactions in the small intestine and their impact on community ecology
  • The role of oral bacteria and small-intestinal consortia in colon and pancreatic cancer
  • Precision microbiome engineering using phage-derived lysins and targeted interventions
  • Functional profiling of butyrate producers and other key gut taxa in health and disease
  • Mobile genetic elements and horizontal gene transfer in complex microbial ecosystems
  • Reproducible pipelines for microbiome, virome and genome-resolved metagenomic analysis

Public Engagement

I am interested in communicating microbiome and phage research to a broad audience, including clinicians, policy makers and non-specialists. This includes explaining how gut microbial ecosystems contribute to cancer risk, how phage-based tools can be used to precisely target bacteria, and how microbiome-informed therapies may be translated into clinical practice.



I also enjoy discussing how we can build locally-informed, globally-relevant microbiome interventions — integrating insights from ecology, evolution, cancer biology and therapeutic development.

Software & Resources

I develop and maintain custom, reproducible pipelines for microbiome and virome analysis, including tools for gene-cluster extraction, region orientation, functional annotation and visualization of bacterial and phage genomes.


Code & Pipelines
